# Schools research — 2026-04-26
Focus: Substitute teachers & temporary staff — what a sub walking into an unfamiliar elementary classroom actually needs at dismissal, and how today's tools fail that workflow.

## TL;DR
- **Subs run ~20% of instructional time post-COVID** (roughly double the pre-pandemic ~10%), and **20% of sub requests go unfilled**. Translation: on any given school day, 1 in 5 classrooms is being closed out at dismissal by either a sub or an over-stretched neighbor teacher. Every dismissal product is implicitly betting that the person calling kids to the car line is the regular teacher; for ~20% of classes that bet is wrong. ([BLS Monthly Labor Review](https://www.bls.gov/opub/mlr/2023/beyond-bls/substitute-teachers-needed-closing-the-gap-on-supply-versus-demand.htm), [Frontline](https://www.frontlineeducation.com/blog/solving-teacher-shortage-crisis/))
- **The universal artifact for sub-on-dismissal is still a paper "sub folder" / "sub binder."** Every elementary-teacher blog tells teachers to leave one with: dismissal chart, photo roster, allergies/medical, IEP/504 notes, drill cheatsheet, bell schedule, aftercare assignments, today's exceptions. The folder is written in August/September and goes stale within weeks because the actual dismissal data lives in the office's app (PikMyKid / SDM / spreadsheet). The wedge is **"print today's sub packet"** — fresh from the live data, on demand, in 30 seconds.
- **PowerSchool's substitute portal is the closest existing "sub identity" pattern, and it's anemic**: per-school shared password + URL (`yourdistrict.com/subs`), sub picks "School → Teacher → enters password," gets attendance + lunch counts. No dismissal data, no photos, no allergies, no exceptions. PikMyKid markets "easy to train a sub" with no actual sub role; SDM "accommodates" subs by having the office adjust the assigned teacher. Nobody in the dismissal category has a first-class sub identity. That's a credible wedge for Pickup Roster.
- **Subs are simultaneously "staff" (Frontline/Aesop/Red Rover) and "visitors" (Raptor) in two different systems that don't talk.** Raptor's own policy is explicit: *"All staff who are not permanently assigned to work in the school building (e.g., IT, maintenance, short-term subs) will sign in as visitors using the Raptor visitor management self-service kiosk."* So the office knows a sub checked in (Raptor), HR knows the absence is filled (Frontline), but the dismissal app has no idea which classroom has a sub today. Bridging that with a 30-second flow at the kiosk → magic-link to that day's dismissal view is a small, valuable feature.
- **Drill procedures change "with little to no notice"**, per Kelly Education's own onboarding doc, and the explicit advice given to subs is *"if you are unable to locate the procedures or the teacher's plans are incomplete, please contact the office or speak to an administrator."* Translation: the system of record for "what does Code Yellow mean here" is "go interrupt the assistant principal." That's the same office-disruption framing that anchors the whole dismissal-software ROI pitch — and it applies just as cleanly to drill cheatsheets.

## Signals
- **Subs cover a doubled share of instructional time and 1 in 5 sub requests is unfilled.** "Since COVID-19 emerged, the percentage of instructional time students spend with substitute teachers has likely doubled to 20%, compared to an estimated 10% before the pandemic." "20 percent of requests for substitutes are unfilled." Snippets attributed to BLS Monthly Labor Review and K-12 Dive. Source: [BLS MLR](https://www.bls.gov/opub/mlr/2023/beyond-bls/substitute-teachers-needed-closing-the-gap-on-supply-versus-demand.htm), [K-12 Dive](https://www.k12dive.com/news/substitute-staffing-challenges-staffed-up/700166/). *Snippet-grounded.*
- **35% of public schools are "extremely concerned" about sourcing subs.** Snippet attributed to Devlin Peck's roundup of teacher-shortage stats. Source: [Devlin Peck](https://www.devlinpeck.com/content/teacher-shortage-statistics). *Snippet-grounded.*
- **The official Kelly Education advice tells subs to physically locate a paper binder or interrupt the office.** Verified against fetched mykelly text: *"The regular classroom teacher will likely have a safety binder or folder in their classroom about drills and/or actual emergencies."* and *"If you are unable to locate the procedures or the teacher's plans are incomplete, please contact the office or speak to an administrator to ensure you are prepared."* Source: [mykelly.com — Emergency procedures](https://www.mykelly.com/career-advice/workplace/emergency-procedures-what-substitute-teachers-need-to-know). *Direct fetch verified.*
- **Drill protocols change with little notice.** Verified mykelly quote: *"Procedures for fire drills, weather-related events, or active shooter situations differ from school to school and can change quickly, over time, with little to no notice."* Source: [mykelly.com — Emergency procedures](https://www.mykelly.com/career-advice/workplace/emergency-procedures-what-substitute-teachers-need-to-know). *Direct fetch verified.*
- **Dismissal is the explicit step the sub is told to "ask the office about."** Verified mykelly quote: *"Check with the school office about dismissal protocol."* and *"Ask what different modes of transportation are available to students and where students need to be for these pickups."* Source: [mykelly.com — Classroom procedures](https://www.mykelly.com/career-advice/workplace/following-classroom-procedures-and-routines-as-substitute-teacher). *Direct fetch verified.*

Lower elementary procedures may have you dismiss each child directly to a trusted adult."* The sub has no way to recognize "trusted adult" without the regular teacher's photo roster + guardian list. Source: [mykelly.com — Classroom procedures](https://www.mykelly.com/career-advice/workplace/following-classroom-procedures-and-routines-as-substitute-teacher). *Direct fetch verified.*
- **The dismissal chart is the artifact subs are universally pointed at.** Snippet: *"They may leave a chart or list that dictates which students use which dismissal method."* "It's easy for substitutes, aides, and guest teachers to see where the students need to go at the end of the day when dismissal charts are posted." Source: [mykelly.com](https://www.mykelly.com/career-advice/workplace/following-classroom-procedures-and-routines-as-substitute-teacher), [jodidurgin.com](https://jodidurgin.com/managing-classroom-dismissal-photos-chart/). *Snippet + fetch verified.*
- **Sub folder content is consistent across every elementary teacher blog**: contact tree, bell schedule, dismissal procedures incl. early/late, emergency procedures (shelter-in-place, active shooter, fire), photo roster with names, allergies, IEPs/504s, behavior notes. Verified educateandrejuvenate quote: *"A sub folder is different than sub plans in that your folder contains all the classroom and school information your substitute will need."* Sources: [educateandrejuvenate.com](https://educateandrejuvenate.com/emergency-sub-folder/), [educateandrejuvenate.com — binder steps](https://educateandrejuvenate.com/create-brilliant-substitute-binder/), [thelearningeffect.com](https://thelearningeffect.com/substitute-binder/). *Direct fetch + snippet verified.*

Source: [powerschool-docs.com](https://ps.powerschool-docs.com/pssis-admin/25.1/substitute-sign-in-permissions), [knowledgeforschools.com](https://www.knowledgeforschools.com/knowledge-base/how-do-i-manage-substitutes-in-powerschool-quick-version/). *Snippet-grounded.*
- **PikMyKid markets sub-friendliness as a feature, not a role.** Snippet: *"The platform is built to be so easy to use that you can train a substitute teacher in your sleep."* No specific sub login, sub mode, sub view documented in marketing copy. - **Sub apps' lesson-plan handoff is unreliable.** Red Rover snippet on the substitute UX: *"Issues have been reported when trying to access lesson plans from classroom teachers, with content often being unpredictable—sometimes working and sometimes not."* Source: [redroverk12.com](https://www.redroverk12.com/blog/the-only-absence-management-and-substitute-teacher-app-youll-ever-need). *Snippet-grounded.* Note: this is about lesson plans, not dismissal — but it's the same broken handoff pattern.
- **Subs may be classified as "guest teachers" in some districts.** Several districts (Linn-Mar, framingham, ASD, Lynwood) and TPT-style sub-binder products use the "guest teacher" framing. Source: [scoot.education](https://scoot.education/7-common-substitute-teacher-problems/) common-problems list. *Snippet-grounded.*
- **First-day disorientation is a documented sub problem.** Snippet (scoot.education): *"It's normal to feel disoriented when first arriving at an unfamiliar school, and arriving late can make a poor first impression with schools less likely to call you back."* And subs *"often receive absolutely zero training or orientation."* Sources: [scoot.education](https://scoot.education/7-common-substitute-teacher-problems/), [Williams Alumni](https://alumni.williams.edu/alumni-career-commentary/the-experience-of-being-a-substitute/). *Snippet-grounded.*

## Feature hypotheses
1. **Sub Packet (print/email today)** — "Sub assigned" event triggers a one-button packet: per-classroom dismissal chart for today (with today's exceptions inlined), photo roster, allergies, IEP/504 flags, drill quick-card, bell schedule, "trusted adult" guardian thumbnails. Office prints or emails it. Replaces the paper sub folder with a fresh-from-live-data version. **User: office admin.** Signal: **strong** (every elementary-teacher blog independently advocates for this artifact and laments how stale it gets). **Build: S–M.** This is mostly a print-view of data we already have, plus a "today's exceptions diff" computation; it could ship as a single PDF route in <2 weeks.
2. **Sub-Mode QR + magic link** — A small printed card per classroom ("If you're subbing here today, scan this QR") → web view scoped to that one classroom for 12 hours, read-only, no account, audit-logged. Shows photo roster + today's exceptions + drill cheatsheet + "tap to ask the office a question." **User: substitute.** Signal: **strong** (PowerSchool's sub portal pattern validates the demand; nobody in dismissal has it). **Build: M.** The hard parts are the time-bound token + audit log + per-classroom scope; the UI is mostly an existing read-only dismissal view.
3. **Today's Exceptions Diff** — Top of every classroom's dismissal screen: a 1-line change set vs. the printed chart on the wall ("Today: 2 changes — Mia is car-rider not bus, Jaden is staying for ASP"). Equally useful for regular teachers but indispensable for subs. **User: teacher + sub.** Signal: **strong** (SDM's most-cited recurring complaint is recurring-events friction, which is the same problem class). **Build: S.** This is a presentation layer over data we already model.
4. **Frontline / Red Rover absence webhook → "sub today"** — When the absence-management vendor posts an "absence filled" event, Pickup Roster flips that classroom into Sub Mode automatically: sub-friendly view becomes the default on the classroom phone, the office gets a notification, the day's packet auto-emails to the sub's known email. **User: office admin + sub.** Signal: **medium** (no public webhook docs surfaced, but the integration story is the standard play with these vendors). **Build: M–L.** Vendor BD is the unknown; if Frontline doesn't expose a webhook, there's a fallback (admin marks classroom as "sub today" in 1 click).
5. **Drill Cheatsheet (live, building-scoped)** — A QR card the sub finds on the classroom door → live URL with this building's most recent drill protocol, "what does this PA code mean," location of the rally point on a building map, and the sub's per-room evacuation roster. The roster is the killer feature — it's the same accountability ask from the 2026-04-23 reunification brief, but framed for the highest-risk user. **User: substitute (and any temporary staff).** Signal: **medium** (mykelly is explicit on this; nobody in dismissal sells it). **Build: M.** Reuses reunification work.
6. **Sub Verification at end of day** — One button on the sub view at 3:30 PM: "All students dismissed correctly." If they tap a different button ("I had a question"), the office gets paged before the sub leaves the building, while the kid is still on campus. Replaces the "we found out at 3:45 that someone got on the wrong bus" failure mode. **User: substitute.** Signal: **medium** (inferred; no source explicitly named this gap). **Build: S.**
7. **Raptor kiosk handoff** — At the visitor kiosk, when "purpose: substitute" is selected, the kiosk prints both the visitor badge AND a slip with a magic link / QR for that day's classroom. Eliminates the "I checked in but I have no idea what classroom I'm in" gap. **User: substitute + front office.** Signal: **medium** (Raptor's policy-doc framing of subs as visitors is explicit; the integration story has to be confirmed). **Build: L.** Likely needs Raptor partnership; revisit after we know how Raptor's kiosk SDK works.

## Competitive notes
- **PikMyKid** uses sub-friendliness as marketing copy ("train in your sleep") but has no documented sub-specific role, sub login, or sub view. The teacher app login is the path; effectively a sub uses the regular teacher's logged-in tablet. *Implication for us:* "first-class sub identity" is differentiable language.
- **School Dismissal Manager** treats subs as a data-edit problem for admins ("adjust for substitute teachers"). Same gap as PikMyKid; the office is doing more work.
- **Schoolhouse Driveline / Carline** (snippet-only) — no sub-specific feature surfaced.
- **Raptor** owns the visitor identity flow and explicitly buckets short-term subs as visitors. They are the natural integration partner here, not a competitor — they don't do dismissal.
- **PowerSchool SIS** has the only real sub portal in the K-12 stack, and it's intentionally narrow (attendance + lunch). Roster data is technically there but not exposed to subs in a useful way.
- **Frontline (Aesop) / Red Rover** own the absence/sub-assignment funnel. They know which sub is at which school today before anyone else does. Webhook integration is the most leveraged piece of vendor BD we could do for this surface area.
