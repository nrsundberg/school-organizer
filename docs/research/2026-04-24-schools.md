# Schools research — 2026-04-24
Focus: Admin onboarding & first-week-of-school setup — what breaks between the PO signature and Day 1, and where current dismissal vendors push work onto office staff.

> Rotation vs. prior days: Day 1 covered car-line wait times and parent-side dismissal-exception UX. Day 2 covered safety drills, accountability, and reunification. Today is the **admin/office-staff pre-launch surface area** — roster import, data verification, staff role setup, and the first two weeks of the school year when churn is highest.

## TL;DR
- The single quantified pain in this whole category is the one that anchors every admin ROI pitch: **secretaries field ~10,000 dismissal-change requests/year and spend up to 2 hours/day on them**, i.e., roughly 45 workdays/year per school on a task that is 100% replaceable by a form submission. Every vendor in the space (PickUp Patrol, SDM, PikMyKid, Schoolhouse Driveline) either cites this stat or a paraphrase of it. Build the office-staff-hours-saved dashboard and you have the renewal conversation pre-written.
- **"We get you live in 10 days"** is PikMyKid's headline onboarding promise, with 5 days when an SIS integration is available (PowerSchool, FACTS, Skyward, Blackbaud, Central Access, or CSV). SDM's version is "a few hours of training and setup time if you have a roster file." For a solo-founder product, the durable wedge is **self-serve onboarding in one afternoon without a sales call** — the small-school buyer doesn't want to schedule a call, they want to upload a CSV and have tags print.
- The single biggest avoidable failure on Day 1 is **parents haven't completed the back-to-school verification packet.** Aeries' own ideas forum has 126 upvotes on a single suggestion asking for inline PDF fill/sign, with a direct admin quote: *"parents print our forms but they just don't bring them in."* An authorized-pickup list the office can trust on Day 1 is the artifact that turns into teacher trust downstream — and nobody ships it well.
- The **roster→transportation→tag→teacher-printout** pipeline is currently patched together out of TPT templates, PowerPoint, sticker paper, laminators, and zip ties. Teachers and secretaries describe this workflow openly as the first-week ritual. Owning that pipeline end-to-end (roster in, printable tags + classroom door sheets out) is a small, sellable feature a single engineer can ship in two weeks.
- Vendor marketing consistently frames first-week chaos as *"staff scrambling to identify cars, locate students, flip through paper rosters, shouting across walkie-talkies."* Translate that into product: the killer Day-1 artifact is a **per-staff-role cheat sheet** — who you are, where you stand, who you're watching for, what to do when something goes wrong — that prints from our roster.

## Signals

- **Secretary workload stat (PickUp Patrol vendor blog, verified):** *"Did you know that many schools process 10,000 dismissal plan changes annually? That means school secretaries can spend up to 2 hours a day just dealing with dismissal plan changes… The time that secretaries spend juggling all of the phone calls and emails can add up to 45 days a year!"* Source: [pickuppatrol.net — Secretary Appreciation Day](https://www.pickuppatrol.net/articles/celebrate-secretary-appreciation-day-with-pickup-patrol) (fetched OK).

- **PikMyKid onboarding pitch:** *"Get everyone onboarded and ready to go within 10 days,"* and per their own messaging, schools with partner integrations (PowerSchool, FACTS, Skyward, Blackbaud, Central Access) or CSV can be live "in as little as 5 days." Source: WebSearch snippet aggregating pikmykid.com onboarding/FAQ pages.

- **PikMyKid implementation blog (fetched OK, dateModified Dec 2025):** *"Without proper onboarding, even the best systems fail to deliver results. Delays, miscommunication, and confusion arise when users aren't fully supported. From login issues to unclear roles, the challenges of poor rollout can quickly overwhelm your school community."* Structured training recommendation: overview session → interactive workshops → role-play drills → quick-reference cheat sheets → pilot group before full deployment. Source: [pikmykid.com/blog/successful-school-dismissal-app](https://www.pikmykid.com/blog/successful-school-dismissal-app) (fetched OK).

- **SDM self-description:** *"If you have a roster file of students and parents, you can be up and running with a few hours of training and set-up time."* Source: WebSearch snippet summarizing schooldismissalmanager.com/HowItWorks.aspx. The implication — and prior-brief reviews back this up — is that the first-hour experience is fine for the admin; the long-tail pain is the parent entering exceptions one child at a time afterwards.

- **Aeries Ideas forum — 126 votes for inline fillable forms (fetched OK):** The full suggestion body says it in exactly these words: *"it would benefit the parent/guardian to make this section a little more user friendly and more efficient if the parents can just click on the PDF form, fill it out, and electronically sign or type in their name,"* with an admin comment directly above it: *"parents print our forms but they just don't bring them in. This would be so much easier if they could electronically sign or type in their name."* Source: [ideas.aeries.com — Online Enrollment / Data Confirmation Fillable Forms](https://ideas.aeries.com/forums/925735-aeries-ideas/suggestions/41316571-online-enrollment-data-confirmation-fillable-f) (fetched OK, 126 votes as of 2026-04-24).

- **Schoolhouse Driveline first-week blog (fetched OK, dateModified Dec 2025) names the five first-week failure modes:** (1) car-line neighborhood spillover because parents arrive early, (2) angry phone calls from parents who don't understand the pickup instructions, (3) *"school staff often scramble to identify cars, locate students, and coordinate with each other. That's where mistakes happen, students get missed or delayed, and stress levels skyrocket… there's no need to shout across walkie-talkies or flip through paper rosters,"* (4) students waiting in hallways / bouncing between staging areas, (5) new families' trust being set by what Day-1 pickup feels like. Source: [schoolhousedriveline.com/5-ways-a-dismissal-app-reduces-chaos-on-the-first-week-of-school](https://schoolhousedriveline.com/5-ways-a-dismissal-app-reduces-chaos-on-the-first-week-of-school/) (fetched OK).

- **WeAreTeachers — Davidson Elementary (Lexington, NC), Principal Paula Jones (fetched OK):** describes the baseline paper-note workflow that a software product is replacing: *"Parents know they must send in written documentation when a change is occurring for pickup purposes, and the office staff then notifies classroom teachers."* She also flags the first-week dependency directly: *"we teach dismissal procedures from the first day. The classroom teachers work hard the first week of school teaching procedures."* Source: [weareteachers.com — School Dismissal Procedures Every Principal Can Manage](https://www.weareteachers.com/school-dismissal-procedure-planning/) (fetched OK).

- **TPT / National School Forms ecosystem (snippet only):** The DIY first-week roster→tag pipeline is a real market. Teachers buy editable bus-rider/car-rider chart templates, print tags in PowerPoint onto sticker paper, laminate, and *zip-tie* tags to backpacks. "An 8.5 x 11 sheet posted near the classroom door notes all bus numbers for each student and can be laminated and used with vis-à-vis markers for easy updates." Signal: there is existing, paid willingness-to-pay for *just the artifacts* (tags + classroom door sheet) independent of any dismissal software. Source: WebSearch snippets from [teacherspayteachers.com bus dismissal browse](https://www.teacherspayteachers.com/browse?search=bus+dismissal) and [nationalschoolforms.com bus tags](https://nationalschoolforms.com/categories/bus-routing-dismissal-tags.html).

- **Clever/ClassLink rostering reality (snippet-verified):** *"Clever does not handle requests beyond logging in users and providing roster and course data."* ClassLink's services are built on OneRoster. Practical translation: **one-way sync, no writebacks**. Any product relying on "we'll sync with your SIS" still needs its own editing UI for authorized-pickup lists and transportation modes, because none of that flows back. Source: [Clever rostering overview](https://www.clever.com/products/rostering), [ClassLink / OneRoster overview via Medium](https://medium.com/@sdhglobal/rostering-integration-to-education-software-classlink-clever-2a6395e4a09f).

- **District back-to-school verification pattern is universal (snippet-verified, multiple districts):** Seattle, Lincoln, Fairfax, Prince William, San Bernardino, and others all run an annual "Back to School Packet" / "Data Confirmation" flow that asks parents to review: emergency contacts, *"Emergency Contacts have your permission to pick your child up from school during the school day,"* phone numbers, health info, policy acknowledgments. Live in SIS parent portals (Aeries, ParentVUE, PowerSchool Parent Portal). These flows exist but (per the Aeries complaint) are clunky enough that completion rates suffer and paper fallbacks dominate. Source: [LPS back-to-school verification](https://home.lps.org/studentservices/back-to-school-verification/), [Fairfax ParentVUE OVU packet](https://www.fcps.edu/family-resources/family-communication-resources/sis-parentvue-online-verification-and-update-packet), [Prince William back-to-school packet](https://www.pwcs.edu/support_services/back_to_school/instructions/index) — all snippet-grounded.

## Feature hypotheses

1. **Self-serve import wizard with named SIS templates** — Detect and map common column layouts from PowerSchool quick-export, Infinite Campus, Aeries, Veracross, Blackbaud, FACTS, Skyward. For schools without an SIS, "paste Google Sheet / upload CSV" with inline column-mapping UI. Goal: admin's *first hour* with the product ends with a live roster, not a support ticket. User: admin. Signal: strong (every competitor's timeline-to-live promise is gated on this step; SDM's "a few hours" and PikMyKid's "10 days" both hinge on the roster-file handoff). Build size: M. Ship-in-two-weeks: yes, if we ignore Clever/ClassLink OneRoster for v1 and do CSV + Google Sheet paste only.

2. **"First-week readiness" checklist with percent-complete** — Dashboard panel that counts, in plain English: roster uploaded (X/Y students), transportation mode set (X/Y), guardians verified (X/Y), authorized pickups added (X/Y), parent invites sent (X/Y), pickup tags printed, teacher door sheets printed. Drives Day-1 preparedness without requiring the admin to read our docs. User: admin + secretary. Signal: strong (implied by every onboarding-blog's framing + the Aeries 126-vote complaint about completion). Build size: S. This is the single highest-leverage feature in this brief — cheap, visible, and it doubles as the renewal artifact.

3. **Inline parent data confirmation (the "kill the paper packet" play)** — Parent logs in once pre-Day-1, verifies name/phone/email, edits emergency contacts, adds authorized pickups with PINs, selects default transportation mode for each child, checks a box on policy acknowledgments. No PDF. No print-and-return. Directly targets the Aeries 126-vote complaint. User: parent + secretary. Signal: strong. Build size: M. Competitive framing: position as *"the back-to-school packet, except it actually gets returned."*

4. **Auto-generated Day-1 teacher classroom-door sheet** — One PDF per teacher the night before Day 1, re-generated any time roster data changes: homeroom list, each student's transportation mode, tag number, guardian phone, allergies, any notes ("has older sibling in 4th," "picked up by grandmother MWF"). Replaces the TPT-template-in-PowerPoint-then-laminate ritual. User: teacher + secretary. Signal: strong (TPT ecosystem is paid evidence of demand; WeAreTeachers/Schoolhouse Driveline both cite the paper-roster-at-the-door pattern). Build size: S. Our existing PDF generator + roster joins will do most of this.

5. **Per-role staff cheat-sheet generator** — Print a one-pager per staff role: "Mrs. Lee — Gate A — watching for tags 101–180 — Grade 2/3. If a tag is called and no car, radio Mrs. K. If a parent arrives without tag, send to office." Built from the roster + a simple role/zone config. Addresses the Schoolhouse Driveline "staff scramble" and PikMyKid "unclear roles" failure modes directly. User: dismissal coordinator + staff. Signal: medium-strong. Build size: S–M.

6. **Roster drift queue for the first three weeks** — Nightly (or on-demand) CSV re-import diffs against the live roster and surfaces adds/withdraws/transportation-mode changes in a review queue instead of overwriting silently. Critical during late enrollment (common in the first two weeks). Prevents the "kid shows up and we have no idea who they are" moment on Week 2, Day 3. User: secretary. Signal: medium. Build size: M.

7. **Family adoption report pre-launch** — Admin dashboard: how many families have logged in, verified info, added authorized pickups, set a default transportation mode, have no email on file. Exportable to CSV so the secretary can make the "please call these 14 families" phone list. Drives completion *before* Day 1. User: admin. Signal: medium. Build size: S.

8. **"Opt me out of this rollout" printable fallback** — For the ~5% of families who won't engage digitally, a single-page paper form that the secretary can scan/photograph to populate the same record. Quiet but real — an adoption product that *doesn't* have a paper fallback will lose one-school-building trust on Day 1 because of that 5%. User: secretary + low-tech family. Signal: medium (inferred from Aeries admin's "parents print our forms but they just don't bring them in" + Day 7 accessibility/low-tech angle hinted in rotation guidance). Build size: S.

## Competitive notes

- **PikMyKid** — 10-day onboarding pitch; 5-day pitch with partner integrations. Own blog admits the friction: *"login issues and unclear roles… can quickly overwhelm your school community."* Recommended rollout path is human-heavy (overview session, workshops, role-play drills, quick-reference guides, pilot). For us, every step they recommend humans run is a feature we could ship (cheat-sheet generator, role config, pilot cohort toggle). Source: pikmykid.com/blog.
- **School Dismissal Manager (SDM)** — Self-positions around "roster file → a few hours of setup." Consistent with prior-brief weakness: the import is fine; the per-child, per-day parent UX is where it bleeds. Day-1 admin experience is probably not the primary SDM wedge for us to attack; per-family ongoing UX is.
- **PickUp Patrol** — Owns the secretary-hours-saved narrative in marketing (the 10,000/2hr/45-day stats). Brand-thin online presence; couldn't fetch product pages at depth. Worth a deeper Day-4 or Day-5 probe on product specifics.
- **Schoolhouse Driveline** — First-week pain is the centerpiece of their marketing. Flat $935/yr per campus (from Day 1 brief); 60-day free trial. Their "5 ways" blog is useful messaging ammo: every one of their bullets maps to a feature hypothesis above.
- **Aeries, PowerSchool Parent Portal, ParentVUE (SIS parent portals, not dismissal apps)** — Own the back-to-school verification flow in most US districts. Because they're SIS-centric, they struggle with UX polish (the 126-vote fillable-forms request is the clearest evidence). A dismissal app that can import from these *and* ship a better verification UX than they do gets a real wedge against both sides — "we save the office hours *and* we make the form completion flow not terrible."
- **Clever / ClassLink** — Rostering rails, not dismissal products. Integration story is one-way and login-scoped. Mentioning "Clever/ClassLink integration" in an admin sales motion is table stakes, not differentiation. Defer v1.
