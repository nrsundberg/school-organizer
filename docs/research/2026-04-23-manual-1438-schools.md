## TL;DR
- The accountability gap that drives every "buy" decision in this category is simple: **during a drill or incident, who is in which room, right now, and who is missing?** Every competitor (Ruvna, Raptor, CrisisGo, Navigate360, PikMyKid) leads with a version of that claim because paper/clipboard rosters still dominate in most schools. This is the strongest wedge available adjacent to dismissal — and it reuses the Pickup Roster data model (roster → child → guardian).
- **Compliance pressure is rising.** Connecticut's SB 298 (Mar 2026) requires trauma-informed drills, advance parent notification, age-appropriate pre-briefs, explicit "this is a drill" callouts, disability accommodations, and a 2028 state study. Minnesota passed similar legislation earlier. Michigan already mandates drill posting within 30 days + 3-year retention. A product that prints a compliant drill log out of the box is suddenly worth real money.
- **The #1 structural problem during drills is unaccounted-for students** — kids in hallways/bathrooms/nurse/pull-out groups when the alarm hits. Every published best-practice doc flags this as the hardest part of accountability. Solving "who is not in their teacher's classroom right now" is the killer feature, and it's where Ruvna's "100% accountability, zero blind spots" copy lives.
- **Substitute teachers are the weak link.** Procedures vary by building, change often, and subs get hours (sometimes minutes) to absorb them. No one is shipping a "sub-ready emergency card" that renders the current school's SRP + nearest exit + today's class roster + pre-assigned buddy classroom. This is a small, sellable feature.
- **Reunification is where Pickup Roster's dismissal DNA is closest to a wedge.** The Standard Reunification Method's verify-parent-ID-then-release flow is structurally the same as guardian-authorized curbside pickup. A school already running us for car-line can flip to "reunification mode" in one tap — a differentiator no pure dismissal app currently executes on.
- **Pricing in this adjacent segment is higher.** Raptor Emergency Management runs ~$2,095/building/yr (reported in a Pennsylvania district procurement). Ruvna is quote-only but serves 300+ schools across 43 states. The flat-fee small-school pricing thesis from Day 1 survives at these price points too, probably better.

## Signals

- **SRP adoption is massive and free.** The "I Love U Guys" Foundation's Standard Response Protocol ("Hold, Secure, Lockdown, Evacuate, Shelter") is used in over 30,000 schools. Materials are free to download; printed books are optional. Any product in this space should speak SRP vocabulary natively. Source: [iloveuguys.org](https://iloveuguys.org/The-Standard-Response-Protocol.html) (fetched OK).

- **Michigan drill mandate is concrete and searchable.** K-12 schools must run 5 evacuation drills/yr (3 by Dec 1), 2 tornado drills (1 in March), and 3 lockdown drills (1 by Dec 1, 1 after Jan 1). Every drill must be posted to the school website within 30 days and retained 3 years. Source: [MSP school safety drills doc](https://www.michigan.gov/-/media/Project/Websites/msp/gcsd/pdfs2/school_safety_drills_documentation__reporting_requirements.pdf) (WebFetch 403; snippet-grounded).

- **Georgia: monthly fire drills when school is in session, plus one within the first 10 days.** Source: [Georgia OCI fire drill guidelines](https://oci.georgia.gov/document/document/2024-2025-schedulepdf/download) via snippet.

- **Connecticut SB 298 (March 2026)** requires trauma-informed drills, advance parent notice, pre-drill age-appropriate instruction, explicit "this is a drill" language, accommodations for students with disabilities, bans assailant simulations during school hours, and commissions a study due July 1, 2028. Quote from Patrice McCarthy (CABE exec dir): "We don't want to traumatize students who may have experienced trauma in other parts of their lives as well." Quote from Abbey Clements (Teachers Unify to End Gun Violence): the law "could be a model for what more states are doing to keep students and staff safe." Source: [CT Mirror](https://ctmirror.org/2026/03/27/school-crisis-drills-in-ct-get-rules-and-standards-with-new-law/) (fetched OK).

- **National teachers unions (AFT + NEA) have called for re-thinking student involvement in lockdown drills** — particularly active-shooter simulations. The live-action, sensory-immersive drills (officers running halls making noise) are specifically cited as traumatic for younger kids. Source: [NPR 2020 coverage](https://www.npr.org/2020/02/11/804468827/2-big-teachers-unions-call-for-rethinking-student-involvement-in-lockdown-drills) via snippet + 2025 follow-up [NPR piece](https://www.npr.org/2025/10/29/nx-s1-5570744/lockdown-drills-have-become-a-routine-part-of-the-school-year-but-should-they-be) (WebFetch timeout; snippet only).

- **Accountability is uneven by drill type.** For fire drills, teachers take attendance at the outside assembly point and report misses to the office. For lockdowns, teachers take attendance "discreetly" once the room is secured — i.e., they stay silent, which kills any phone-call or radio report and forces async reporting. Any app in this space needs a silent-mode workflow. Source: snippets from [Texas SSC SRP toolkit](https://txssc.txstate.edu/tools/srp-toolkit/lockdown), [BeSafe best practices](https://www.besafe.net/news/school-safety-drills-a-collection-of-best-practices-and-procedures/).

- **The hardest part of accountability is the students not in their primary classroom.** Pull-out services, bathrooms, cafeteria, nurse, library, specials — all are named as gaps in official best-practice docs. Teachers maintain "add/drop" lists in real time for unexpected visitors. Source: snippets from [Ruvna](https://www.ruvna.com/products/accountability), [Navigate360 blog](https://navigate360.com/blog/navigate360-app-for-school-safety-drills/), [Texas SSC](https://txssc.txstate.edu/tools/srp-toolkit/drills).

- **Ruvna's public testimonial: "Our unexpected fire drill last Friday was completed in 4 minutes — and this was at lunchtime and with a large group of non-students and parents on campus!"** (Principal, unnamed.) Ruvna claims 300+ schools across 43 states and "100% accountability, zero blind spots." Pricing is quote-only. Reviewers cite the SIS integration as the unlock and ask for more customizable attendance reports. Source: [ruvna.com/products/accountability](https://www.ruvna.com/products/accountability) (fetched OK), [Capterra Ruvna page](https://www.capterra.com/p/196484/Ruvna/) (snippet).

- **Raptor Emergency Management pricing is public enough to anchor positioning.** Lehighton Area SD (PA) signed 3 site licenses at $9,310 total (15 months, $3,025 one-time + $6,285 recurring) — ~$2,095/building/yr. Source: [Times News Online (LASD)](https://www.tnonline.com/20250702/lasd-buys-subscription-for-emergency-system/) via snippet; also referenced in [Capterra Raptor page](https://www.capterra.com/p/187616/Raptor-Emergency-Management/).

- **PikMyKid already bundles Emergency Reunification** alongside dismissal. Reviews flag: app occasionally crashes at peak (dismissal), learning curve for setup, and a requested feature — automatic notification to the other parent + emergency contacts when a child isn't picked up on time. That unmet request is a good concrete feature to steal. Source: [G2 PikMyKid reviews](https://www.g2.com/products/pikmykid/reviews), [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/165709/PikMyKid/), [justuseapp aggregation](https://justuseapp.com/en/app/877617948/pikmykid/reviews) via snippet.

- **The Standard Reunification Method's choreography is a 5-step ID + scan loop** (student check-in at holding area → student exit scan → guardian check-in → pass printed → QR scanned at final handoff with guardian initials). Two staging areas minimum; three recommended. The structural overlap with a curbside-pickup PIN + tag workflow is high. Source: [CENTEGIX reunification guide](https://www.centegix.com/blog/how-to-create-an-effective-school-reunification-plan/) (fetched OK, vendor-authored).

- **Substitutes repeatedly self-report as underprepared.** myKelly (substitute-staffing blog): "Procedures for fire drills, weather-related events, or active shooter situations differ from school to school and can change quickly, over time, with little to no notice." Guidance defaults to "ask the main office" — i.e., there's no single source of truth the sub can load on arrival. Source: [myKelly](https://www.mykelly.com/emergency-procedures-what-substitute-teachers-need-to-know/) via snippet.

- **Parent notification in non-English languages is a known capability gap for emergency alerts.** ReachWell advertises 130-language emergency alerts and partnerships with emergency agencies for natural-disaster shelter-in-place orders. TalkingPoints does two-way translation for routine teacher-parent comms. Neither is purpose-built for a drill/emergency roster app. Source: [ReachWell](https://www.reachwellapp.com/apps-are-helping-teachers-communicate-with-families-that-dont-speak-english), [Chalkbeat](https://www.chalkbeat.org/colorado/2024/03/13/phone-app-removing-language-barriers-from-teacher-parent-communications/) via snippet.

- **After-action reports repeatedly surface the same failure modes**: missing/outdated equipment, weak evacuation routes, staff-signal confusion, and procedural (not technical) communication failures — "mixed messages, slow response times, inconsistent media coordination." Source: [AlertMedia AAR guide](https://www.alertmedia.com/blog/after-action-report/), [REMS AAR fact sheet](https://rems.ed.gov/docs/AfterActionReportsFactSheet_508C.pdf) via snippet.

## Feature hypotheses

1. **Drill Accountability ("who is with whom, right now")** — Teacher taps each student present on a pre-synced roster; the office screen fills in as rooms report. Pull-out / nurse / specialist rosters surface automatically (pre-mapped by period). Missing students bubble to an incident dashboard with last-known location (SIS + attendance + visitor log). User: teacher + incident commander. Signal: **strong** (direct parity with Ruvna/Raptor core; paper-rosters-are-still-default in many schools). Build size: **M**. This is the main wedge into the safety-drill segment; reuses our roster/child/guardian model almost unchanged.

2. **Silent Lockdown Mode** — Dark-UI, one-hand-operable roster that a teacher can mark from under a desk. No sounds, no vibrations, no push notifications from the app (to avoid giving away a hiding location). Auto-enters this mode when an admin triggers a Lockdown action. User: teacher. Signal: **strong** (best-practice docs explicitly require silent/discreet attendance). Build size: **S**. Differentiates against competitors whose UI was not designed for silence.

3. **Compliant Drill Log Export** — One-click PDF/CSV export of every drill in a configurable window with state-required fields (date, type, duration, weather, participating staff/students, deficiencies, corrective actions) preformatted per state. Auto-posts to an optional public page for states (e.g., Michigan) that mandate it. User: principal + district safety coordinator. Signal: **strong** (MI, GA, CT, CA all have concrete annual requirements; CT just added more). Build size: **S–M**. Easy admin-sale upgrade on top of (1).

4. **Trauma-Informed Drill Notice** — Automated pre-drill parent notification with plain-language "a drill is scheduled on Tuesday at 10:15 — here's what your child will experience, here's how to talk about it, here's the opt-out form if your state allows it." Available in 20+ languages out of the box. User: principal's office → parents. Signal: **medium-strong** (MN and CT now mandate advance notification; national unions pushing for it). Build size: **S**. Cheap differentiator that maps directly onto new law.

5. **Substitute-Ready Emergency Card** — When a sub is assigned to a classroom for the day, the app generates a 1-page PDF / mobile card: today's roster with photos, nearest two exits, pre-assigned buddy classroom, today's SRP color, principal's direct line, and any pull-out schedule for students who'll leave the room. Prints on arrival or opens on the sub's phone via a single QR sign-in. User: substitute + front office. Signal: **medium** (repeatedly flagged in sub-staffing blogs; no competitor ships this that I can find). Build size: **S**.

6. **Reunification Mode (dismissal → crisis pivot)** — Admin flips the already-running dismissal flow into Standard Reunification Method mode: student holding-area check-in, guardian ID verification, pass print, QR-scanned handoff. Because our guardian-PIN + tag-assignment system already models authorized pickup, the pivot is largely a UI overlay on existing data. User: dismissal coordinator + office. Signal: **strong** (CENTEGIX/I-Love-U-Guys choreography maps 1:1 to our existing model; no other dismissal-first vendor ships this cleanly). Build size: **M**.

7. **"Not in room" Bubble-Up** — Real-time dashboard for the incident commander showing students who have not been checked in by any teacher, with their last attended period / location from the SIS, and which other staff should know (ESL pull-out teacher, speech, nurse). One-click to see their guardian's phone number. User: principal / incident commander. Signal: **medium-strong** (this is the specific "missing kid" gap every after-action report cites). Build size: **M**. Builds on (1); worth calling out separately for product framing.

## Competitive notes

- **Ruvna** — Direct K-12 focus. Claims 300+ schools, 43 states. Product line includes Accountability, Attendance, Visitor Management (now free), Notifications. SIS-integrated, clipboard-replacement framing. Pricing quote-only. Reviewer-cited con: customizable attendance reporting is limited. Sales motion: principal-level, long onboarding (reviews call it "comprehensive and individualized"). Closest direct competitor for (1)/(7).
- **Raptor Technologies (Emergency Management module)** — Bundled with VisitorSafe + StudentSafe. ~$2,095/building/yr reported (PA). Sold into big districts; procurement-driven. Has dynamic mapping + first-responder integration — feature set we shouldn't try to match at this price tier.
- **CrisisGo** — Emergency notification + drill workflow. Notable snippet-cited weakness: "only feature CrisisGo doesn't have is real-time building attendance information." That's exactly the wedge (1) targets.
- **Navigate360** — Confusingly named; search results mix the K-12 safety product with an (unrelated?) higher-ed student CRM. K-12 side ships drill-tracking + mapping. Less reviewer noise than Ruvna; worth deeper dive another day.
- **PikMyKid** — Already bundles Emergency Reunification with dismissal/hall-pass/panic. Reviewer complaints specifically about crashes at peak dismissal create an implicit parallel risk for their reunification module — which is load-sensitive at the exact worst moment. Opportunity to position on reliability.
- **CENTEGIX** — Wearable panic buttons, not a pure software competitor. But they publish some of the most detailed reunification playbooks; we can cite their guidance and out-execute on the roster/check-in side.
- **I Love U Guys Foundation / SRP** — Not a vendor but de facto standard. Free materials, 30,000+ school adoption. Building on SRP vocabulary is table stakes.
- **ReachWell, TalkingPoints** — Adjacent translation/parent-comms apps. Not drill tools, but their 130-language claim is a bar we'd need to match (or cleanly integrate with) to credibly serve multilingual-heavy districts.
