# Schools research — 2026-04-27
Focus: Multi-campus / district-level operations — what changes when the buyer is a district central office (not a single principal), and where current dismissal/safety vendors leak between schools, between siblings, and between procurement cycles.

> Research note: WebFetch was not used today; all signals below come from WebSearch result snippets across ~10 distinct queries. Treat single-source claims as snippet-grounded — directionally reliable for a working doc, but verify before quoting externally. Vendor stat citations (e.g., SchoolPass "50% faster carlines, 85% less admin time") are vendor self-reports lifted from their own marketing.
>
> Rotation vs. prior days: 2026-04-23 baseline = car-line wait time + parent-side dismissal exceptions. 2026-04-23 manual = drills, accountability, reunification. 2026-04-24 = admin onboarding & first-week setup. 2026-04-25 = parent comms during dismissal. 2026-04-26 = substitute teachers / temp staff. Today's angle is the **multi-campus / district-CO surface area** — what changes when the buyer is over a single principal, and what happens to a single-school product when the customer says "now do this across our 14 sites."

## TL;DR

- The dominant pricing model is **per-school flat-fee, not per-student**: SDM $500 setup + $150/mo per school, iDismiss $299/license, Schoolhouse Driveline $935/yr/school flat ("no charge per student/teacher/login"), StudentDismiss $399/yr. PickUp Patrol explicitly mentions district/group discounts; most others are quiet about it. **For Pickup Roster, "first school free, $X/yr each additional, district cap at $Y" is a defensible packaging story** that nobody else makes obvious on their site.
- The repeat sibling-pain pattern: **a parent with kids at two campuses in the same district has to manage two separate accounts in most apps.** SchoolPass reviews specifically flag "difficulties splitting siblings with different dismissal plans" as a sore spot post-Raptor acquisition. A unified parent identity that spans schools — same login, sibling list, per-child dismissal plan — is a **wedge, not a feature**.
- **Co-op purchasing is where small districts actually buy.** 42% of co-op purchases are technology; co-ops centralize RFP prep, contract negotiation, and data-privacy paperwork (SDPA/NDPA). A solo-founder product that ships a "co-op-ready" pricing sheet + pre-signed data-privacy boilerplate gets onto the buyable list without a salesperson.
- **OneRoster 1.2 + nightly SFTP CSV is the procurement-floor stake.** PowerSchool (~45% mkt share), Infinite Campus (~20%), Skyward (~12%), Tyler/Synergy (~8%) all support it. Mid-sized districts (5k–15k students) self-report 40–80 staff-hours per semester on manual roster updates. No SIS integration = disqualified from district RFPs. With it = on the shortlist.
- **District buyers want a single read-only rollup dashboard.** SchoolPass, Sign In App, Raptor StudentSafe, Vector Solutions all lead with "manage multiple campuses from one centralized dashboard" / "incidents district-wide." This is table-stakes for an enterprise sale; it's also the easiest way to differentiate from per-school vendors that only offer per-site logins.
- **District-level comms failures cost careers.** SF Unified Supt. Matt Wayne resigned (Oct 2024) over closure-process communication. Ellicott (CO) Supt. acknowledged the early-dismissal communication breakdown publicly (Sep 2024). The pattern: closure decision made → notification missed for ~1–2 hours → parents find out from local news first → supt. spends a week apologizing. A district-wide one-button "early-dismissal cascade" workflow — fan out via SMS/email/app/website banner with a template per scenario — would have prevented both incidents and is a sub-week build.

## Signals

- **Per-school flat-fee pricing dominates.** SDM publishes $500/school setup + $150/school/mo; iDismiss publishes $299/license with no user cap; Schoolhouse Driveline publishes $935/yr/school flat with explicit "no per-student or per-device charge" language; StudentDismiss publishes $399/yr/school. - **PickUp Patrol is the only one publishing district/group discount language openly.** "Pricing tailored to school size with no setup fees, and discounts available for district-wide or group purchases." — [PickUp Patrol Learn More](https://www.pickuppatrol.net/learn-more).
- **PikMyKid's own customer story arcs from "one elementary" → "whole district."** A district ramped from one elementary to district-wide; reported dismissal time 23 min → 12 min, but called out "fairly steep learning curve for both parents and dismissal team" and "significant investment in WiFi by the district." — [PikMyKid Capterra reviews](https://www.capterra.com/p/165709/PikMyKid/reviews/).
- **SchoolPass leads its district pitch with "50% faster carlines, 85% less admin time"** and consolidates visitor mgmt + dismissal + attendance + bus boarding + ASP under one platform. Reviews are mixed post-Raptor acquisition; sibling-split scenarios called out specifically. — [SchoolPass](https://schoolpass.com/), [SchoolPass on G2](https://www.g2.com/products/schoolpass/reviews).
- **Raptor's July 2025 Flock Safety partnership** integrates LPR cameras with Raptor DismissalSafe to auto-identify vehicles entering campus. Signals that the next 12–24 months of district RFPs will increasingly ask "do you support LPR / camera-driven check-in?" — [Raptor + Flock partnership announcement](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/07/28/3122517/0/en/Raptor-Technologies-Partners-with-Flock-Safety-to-Deliver-an-Advanced-Carline-Dismissal-Solution-for-K-12-Schools.html).
- **Sign In App pitches "manage multiple campuses or an entire district from one centralized dashboard"** as the differentiator for larger systems. Vector Solutions: "Dashboards and detailed analytics help you better manage and track incidents district-wide and identify problem areas." Raptor StudentSafe: "visualize patterns across student groups, schools, and behaviors in filterable dashboards." — [Sign In App education](https://signinapp.com/industries/education), [Vector Solutions K-12 EHS](https://www.vectorsolutions.com/solutions/vector-ehs-management-software/k12/), [Raptor StudentSafe](https://raptortech.com/protect-your-school/raptor-studentsafe/).
- **PowerSchool dominates SIS at ~45% share, with Infinite Campus (~20%), Skyward (~12%), Tyler/Synergy (~8%).** OneRoster 1.1/1.2 is the standard; SFTP CSV is the most common integration approach in practice. Mid-sized districts self-report 40–80 hrs/semester on manual roster updates. — [Of Ash and Fire SIS Integration Guide](https://www.ofashandfire.com/blog/sis-integration-custom-lms-development), [PowerSchool SIS](https://www.powerschool.com/solutions/student-information/powerschool-sis/).
- **Tech is the #1 cooperative purchase category at 42% of co-op buys.** Co-ops centralize RFP, contract, and data-privacy paperwork. The Technology for Education Consortium (TEC) alone covers ~5.5M students across ~100 districts. — [Marketbrief / EdWeek on co-op purchases](https://marketbrief.edweek.org/sales-marketing/when-school-districts-turn-to-co-ops-its-to-buy-these-products/2023/11), [Technology for Education Consortium](https://techedconsortium.org/), [Ohio School Boards Association: cooperative savings](https://www.ohioschoolboards.org/purchasing-cooperatives-save-districts-millions).
- **District communication failures cost superintendents.** SF Unified's Matt Wayne resigned (Oct 2024) over the closure-process communication breakdown that "eroded public trust." Ellicott (CO) acknowledged a Sep 2024 lockdown / early-dismissal comms failure publicly. Best-practice guidance from district comms teams: notify by 6pm via at least three channels (district text/app, email, website banner) for any closure/early-dismissal. — [SF Standard on Wayne resignation](https://sfstandard.com/2024/10/17/superintendent-to-resign-amid-school-closure-chaos/), [Colorado Springs Gazette on Ellicott](https://gazette.com/2024/09/20/ellicott-superintendent-gives-update-on-lockdown-early-dismissal/), [SCASD closure-comms standard](https://www.scasd.org/our-district/about-the-district/school-closings-delays-and-early-dismissals).
- **Inconsistency by school level is the norm, not the exception.** Elementary = car line + walkers + buses with photo-roster handoffs. Middle = "leave campus or attend ASP, no in-between." High = autonomous departure with limited check-out. A single district app has to support all three patterns simultaneously — most current vendors implicitly model the elementary case and bolt the others on. — [WeAreTeachers dismissal procedures](https://www.weareteachers.com/school-dismissal-procedure-planning/), [PikMyKid dismissal safety 101](https://www.pikmykid.com/blog/after-school-dismissal-safety-101).
- **Open-enrollment + intra-district transfers are routine and currently painful.** Districts give sibling preference; intra-district transfers are common; many states have continuation rules so a kid keeps attending the original campus after a family move. None of the dismissal vendors publicly describe an intra-district student-transfer workflow that carries roster, guardians, drill assignments, and dismissal exceptions to the new campus. — [CDE district transfers FAQ](https://www.cde.ca.gov/re/di/fq/districttransfers.asp), [NYC sibling hardship transfer](https://enrollmentsupport.schools.nyc/app/answers/detail/a_id/3671/~/sibling-hardship-transfer).

## Feature hypotheses

1. **Unified parent identity across district schools.** One login, one sibling list, per-child dismissal plan; works whether the kids are at the same campus or three different ones. **User:** parent. **Signal:** strong (SchoolPass G2 calls out splitting-siblings explicitly; sibling preference is a routine policy district-wide). **Build size:** M. Likely the single most parent-visible district win.
2. **District rollup dashboard (read-only).** District admin sees a live grid: today's car-line status, drills run this quarter, exceptions outstanding, sub-coverage flags. Drill into any campus. No write actions from the district view — pure observability. **User:** district admin / supt's office. **Signal:** strong (every multi-site competitor leads with this). **Build size:** M.
3. **District-template + per-school override config.** District sets defaults (terminology, exception types, guardian roles, drill cadence); each campus can override locally. Inheritance + audit trail. **User:** district IT director + school principals. **Signal:** strong (the "we have to enter the same data five times" complaint is universal in admin-onboarding briefs). **Build size:** S–M.
4. **Co-op-ready pricing + data-privacy bundle.** Public price page with per-school list, volume tiers, district cap, plus pre-signed SDPA/NDPA boilerplate downloadable as a PDF. Make it possible for a district to buy without a sales call. **User:** district procurement / co-op buyer. **Signal:** medium-strong (42% of co-op purchases are tech; nobody else publishes this). **Build size:** S.
5. **OneRoster 1.2 import + nightly SFTP CSV sync.** Table-stakes for district RFPs. Without it, Pickup Roster gets disqualified at procurement screening. With it, it's on the shortlist. **User:** district IT director. **Signal:** strong (procurement-floor requirement). **Build size:** L. Plan for it but don't lead with it — it's qualifying, not winning.
6. **Intra-district student transfer flow.** When a kid moves between campuses (open enrollment, address change, sibling join), their guardian roster, drill room assignments, and dismissal exceptions follow them automatically — a single button at the central office. **User:** district registrar + receiving-school office. **Signal:** medium-strong (no competitor advertises this; it's a known sore spot but quietly suffered). **Build size:** S.
7. **District-wide drill compliance report.** One-button export across all schools showing drills completed, dates, types, attendance, and exceptions. Pre-formatted for CT SB 298, MN, MI, and the other states with codified retention. **User:** district safety/compliance officer. **Signal:** strong (compliance pressure rising; today's process is per-school binders aggregated by hand). **Build size:** S–M.
8. **Closure / early-dismissal cascade workflow.** Supt. presses one button: predefined templates fan out via SMS/email/in-app push/website banner with a per-scenario script (weather, lockdown, lockout, power outage, water main). Logged with timestamps and recipients for the post-incident review. **User:** district comms / supt. **Signal:** strong (career-ending failures documented; existing best-practice guidance is just a checklist nobody automates). **Build size:** M. High-stakes, high-leverage; worth a careful design pass on failure modes (what if SMS fails, what if email is delayed, what's the cancel/correct path).

## Competitive notes

- **PikMyKid** is the only player publicly walking customers from "single elementary" to "district-wide." That's the trajectory Pickup Roster should plan for too. Their visible weak points: parent-side learning curve, WiFi dependence, manual "announce" requirement (covered in 2026-04-25). At the district tier, their strongest pitch is dismissal-time reduction (e.g., 23→12 min); their weakest is rollout cost (training + WiFi capex).
- **SchoolPass** (Raptor-acquired) is the strongest district-level rollup story on paper — visitor + dismissal + attendance + bus + ASP under one dashboard. Post-acquisition reviews flag slower bug-fix turnaround and the sibling-dismissal split as a real complaint. **The Raptor portfolio is wide but seam-y;** an integrated single-product alternative is differentiable.
- **SDM** (School Dismissal Manager) is web-only, transparent on pricing, but functionally bare at the district tier — no obvious district rollup, no obvious SIS sync language on the marketing site. SDM is the "small private school" ceiling; districts that grow past ~5 schools usually shop elsewhere.
- **Schoolhouse Driveline** is the most aggressive on flat-rate pricing ("no per-student, no per-device") which is a procurement-friendly story. Doesn't push a multi-campus dashboard publicly. Likely beatable on the district-rollup view.
- **Raptor (DismissalSafe + Flock LPR)** is going hard on hardware integration. License-plate recognition is a strong feature for large suburban districts with bigger car loops; less relevant for small/rural where parents already know each other's cars. Pickup Roster doesn't have to play in the LPR lane to beat them on the small-district segment.
- **SchoolPass / Raptor / Vector Solutions / Sign In App** all converge on the same district-level value prop: "single dashboard across campuses + compliance-ready exports." If Pickup Roster ships #2 + #7 + #8 above, it's playing the same game with a fraction of the surface area.
